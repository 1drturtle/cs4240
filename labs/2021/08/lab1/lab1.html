Question 1 Given that light travels at 2.9979e8 (that's 2.9979*108 in scientific notation) meters per second, figure out how fast light moves in miles per second. Then convert this to miles per hour.

>>> light_speed = 2.9979E8
>>> light_speed_centimeters = light_speed * 100
>>> light_speed_inches = light_speed_centimeters / 2.54
>>> light_speed_feet = light_speed_inches / 12
>>> light_speed_miles = light_speed_feet / 5280
>>> light_speed_mph = light_speed_miles * (60 * 60)  # seconds -> minutes -> hours
>>> light_speed_mph
670611130.9949893

Question 2 Tell the time it take for light to go from the sun to the earth if the mean distance of the sun to the earth is 93.0 million miles.

>>> light_speed_mph = 670611130.9949893  # derived from previous problem
>>> sun_distance = 93 * 1_000_000
>>> time_in_hours = sun_distance / light_speed_mph
