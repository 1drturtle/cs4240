Question 1 Given that light travels at 2.9979e8 (that's 2.9979*108 in scientific notation) meters per second, figure out how fast light moves in miles per second. Then convert this to miles per hour.

>>> light_speed = 2.9979E8
>>> light_speed_centimeters = light_speed * 100
>>> light_speed_inches = light_speed_centimeters / 2.54
>>> light_speed_feet = light_speed_inches / 12
>>> light_speed_miles = light_speed_feet / 5280
>>> light_speed_mph = light_speed_miles * (60 * 60)  # hours * (60s * 60m)
>>> light_speed_mph
670611130.9949893

Question 2 Tell the time it take for light to go from the sun to the earth if the mean distance of the sun to the earth is 93.0 million miles.

>>> light_speed_mph = 670611131  # derived from previous problem, rounded
>>> sun_distance = 93 * 1_000_000
>>> time_in_hours = sun_distance / light_speed_mph
>>> time_in_mins = time_in_hours * 60
>>> time_in_mins
8.320768537914411

Question 3 Use the fact that a liter of water weighs one kilogram and that one gallon of water weighs 8.33 lbs to determine the number of cubic inches in a gallon and the number of pounds in a cubic foot of water.

>>> # cubic inches per gallon
>>> gallon_water = 8.33
>>> kg_water = 8.33 / 2.204
>>> cubic_centimeters = kg_water * 1000  # 1 kg = 1 L
>>> cubic_inches = cubic_centimeters / 2.54
>>> cubic_inches
1487.988910642068
>>> # pounds per cubic foot of water
>>> cubic_inches = 12 ** 3
>>> cubic_centimeters = cubic_inches * (2.54 ** 3)
>>> water_kg = cubic_centimeters / 1000
>>> water_lb = water_kg * 2.204
>>> water_lb
62.41032988876802

Question 4 Let us assume that humans weigh an average of 140 lbs and that humans have about the same density as water. If the population of the earth is 7.0 billion humans, estimate the total volume of humanity in cubic miles. Do you find this counterintuitive?

>>> human_weight_lb = 140
>>> human_weight_kg = human_weight_lb / 2.204
>>> human_volume_cm3 = human_weight_kg * 1000  # 1 kg of water is 1L, 1L = 1000 cm^3
>>> all_human_volume_cm3 = human_volume_cm3 * 7E9
>>> all_human_volume_in3 = all_human_volume_cm3 / (2.54 ** 3)
>>> all_human_volume_ft3 = all_human_volume_in3 / (12 ** 3)
>>> all_human_volume_mi3 = all_human_volume_ft3 / (5280 ** 3)
>>> all_human_volume_mi3
0.10667627196340702
This is counterintuitive because the humans would be crushed, and it's not realistically possible to cram all of humanity in such a small space as there would be holes and other problems.
